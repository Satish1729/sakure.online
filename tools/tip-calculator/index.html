Global Tipping

Tipping norms vary: 15–20% is standard in the US. In India, 10% or rounding up is common. Japan rarely expects tips.

Service Quality

5% = poor service, 10% = average, 15% = good, 20%+ = excellent. Adjust based on your experience.

Delivery Tips

For food delivery, 10–15% or ₹20–50 flat is common. Consider tipping more for long distances or bad weather.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I tip at a restaurant? +
In India, 10% is considered standard. In the US, 15–20% is customary. For exceptional service, 25% or more is appreciated. The right amount depends on your location and the quality of service.
Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax amount? +
Most people tip on the pre-tax amount, but either is acceptable. This calculator uses the bill amount you enter, so enter either the pre-tax or total based on your preference.
How do I split the bill fairly? +
Enter the total bill (including what everyone ordered), choose a tip %, and enter the number of people. This calculator divides the total + tip equally. For unequal splits, divide by each person's portion separately.
