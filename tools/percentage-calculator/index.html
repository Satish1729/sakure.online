Basic Formula

Percentage = (Part / Total) × 100. Simply divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100.

📈

% Increase

% Increase = ((New − Old) / Old) × 100. A positive result means growth.

💡

Quick Tip

To find 10% of any number, just move the decimal one place left. 10% of 350 = 35.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a percentage of a number? +
Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For example, 25% of 80 = (25 × 80) / 100 = 20. Use the "X% of Y" tab above for instant results.
How do I find what percentage one number is of another? +
Divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100. Example: 30 out of 150 = (30 / 150) × 100 = 20%. Use the "X is what % of Y?" tab.
What is percentage increase/decrease? +
Percentage change = ((New Value − Old Value) / Old Value) × 100. A positive result is an increase, negative is a decrease.
How do I find the original value before a percentage was added? +
Use the Reverse Percentage tab. Original = Final Value / (1 + percentage/100). E.g., if price after 20% increase is ₹120, original = 120 / 1.2 = ₹100.
