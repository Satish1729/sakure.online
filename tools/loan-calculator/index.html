Frequently Asked Questions

What is EMI? +
EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment made by a borrower to a lender each month. It includes both a principal repayment component and interest on the outstanding loan balance.
How is EMI calculated? +
EMI = [P × r × (1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ − 1] where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100), n = total months. The formula calculates equal monthly payments that fully amortize the loan.
What factors affect my EMI? +
Three factors: Loan Amount (higher loan = higher EMI), Interest Rate (higher rate = higher EMI), and Tenure (longer tenure = lower EMI but more total interest paid).
How can I reduce my EMI? +
You can reduce EMI by: making a larger down payment, choosing a longer tenure, negotiating a lower interest rate, or making prepayments to reduce the outstanding principal.
