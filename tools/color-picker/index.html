Color Formats Explained

HEX is the most common web color format (#RRGGBB). RGB specifies red, green, and blue channel values from 0-255. HSL uses hue (0-360°), saturation (0-100%), and lightness (0-100%) — often more intuitive for designers because adjusting lightness creates natural shades and tints.

WCAG Contrast Guidelines

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines require a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text (AA level) and 7:1 for enhanced contrast (AAA level). Large text (18px+ or 14px+ bold) needs 3:1 for AA and 4.5:1 for AAA. This tool checks your color against both white and black backgrounds to help you choose accessible color combinations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert HEX to RGB?

Each pair of hex digits represents a color channel: #RRGGBB. Convert each pair from hexadecimal to decimal. For example, #6C72FF → R:108, G:114, B:255. This tool does it automatically — just paste any HEX code.

What are complementary colors?

Complementary colors sit opposite each other on the color wheel (180° apart in hue). They create high contrast and visual tension when used together. Analogous colors are adjacent on the color wheel (30° apart) and create harmonious, unified palettes.
