What is BMI?

BMI is a simple calculation using height and weight to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems.

⚠️

BMI Limitations

BMI doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat. Athletes may have high BMI despite being healthy. Always consult a doctor.

🎯

Healthy Range

A BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered normal/healthy for most adults. Children use different age-specific charts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is BMI calculated? +
BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)². For example, if you weigh 70 kg and are 1.75 m tall: BMI = 70 / (1.75²) = 70 / 3.0625 ≈ 22.9.
Is BMI accurate for everyone? +
BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic measure. It doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age, sex, or fat distribution. Consult a healthcare professional for a comprehensive assessment.
What BMI is considered healthy? +
For adults, a BMI of 18.5–24.9 is considered normal weight. Below 18.5 is underweight, 25–29.9 is overweight, and 30+ is obese. These thresholds may vary by ethnicity.
How does BMI differ for children? +
For children (2–19 years), BMI is age- and sex-specific, reported as a percentile. A BMI in the 5th–85th percentile is considered healthy for children.
