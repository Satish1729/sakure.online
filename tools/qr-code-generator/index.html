QR Code Types

URL QR codes open a website when scanned. WiFi QR codes allow one-scan network connection on smartphones (no manual password entry needed — great for guests and businesses). Email QR codes open a pre-filled email compose window. Phone QR codes initiate a call. SMS QR codes open a pre-filled text message. Plain text QR codes display any custom content.

Tips for Better QR Codes

Ensure high contrast between the QR code and background colors for reliable scanning. Test your QR code with multiple scanner apps before printing. For print materials, use at least 512×512 pixels or SVG format for scalability. Add a small margin (quiet zone) around the QR code — most scanners need this padding to detect the code reliably.

What's the difference between PNG and SVG?

PNG is a pixel-based image at your chosen resolution — good for screen use and small prints. SVG is a vector format that scales to any size without quality loss — ideal for large prints like posters, banners, and signage.
