How to Calculate GST in India (Step-by-Step Guide)

Whether you're a business owner raising an invoice or a buyer checking if you've been charged correctly — here's exactly how GST is calculated, with plain examples.

What is GST?

GST stands for Goods and Services Tax. It is a unified indirect tax that replaced a tangle of older taxes (VAT, service tax, excise duty, etc.) across India when it was introduced in July 2017. GST is collected at every stage of the supply chain, but the final tax burden lands on the end consumer.

GST applies to the supply of goods and services across India. It is administered by the GST Council and is split between the central government and state governments depending on the type of transaction.

GST rates in India

India uses a four-slab GST structure:

  • 5% — Essential goods: packaged food, healthcare items, economy class air travel
  • 12% — Standard goods: processed food, business class air travel, mobile phones
  • 18% — Most services and manufactured goods: restaurants, electronics, software
  • 28% — Luxury and sin goods: cars, tobacco, aerated drinks, casinos

Some items like fresh produce, milk, and educational services are exempt from GST (0% rate).

How to calculate GST (simple explanation)

There are two common scenarios:

  • Adding GST — You know the base price and want to find the final price after GST is applied.
  • Removing GST — You have a GST-inclusive price and want to work out the original base price.

Both use straightforward arithmetic — no complex formulas required.

Add GST example

You provide a service worth ₹5,000 and it attracts 18% GST. What should you bill the client?

Base Amount = ₹5,000 GST Rate = 18% GST Amount = ₹5,000 × 18 ÷ 100 = ₹900 Total Billable = ₹5,000 + ₹900 = ₹5,900

Formula: Total = Base × (1 + Rate / 100)

Remove GST example

You receive an invoice for ₹11,800 inclusive of 18% GST. What was the original price before tax?

GST-Inclusive Amount = ₹11,800 GST Rate = 18% Base Amount = ₹11,800 ÷ (1 + 18 ÷ 100) = ₹11,800 ÷ 1.18 = ₹10,000 GST Amount = ₹11,800 − ₹10,000 = ₹1,800

Formula: Base = Inclusive Amount ÷ (1 + Rate / 100)

Common mistakes

  • Applying GST on the wrong base — GST is always calculated on the base (pre-tax) amount, not on a price that already includes tax. Applying 18% on an 18%-inclusive figure gives a wrong (higher) result.
  • Using the wrong slab — Always verify the correct GST rate for the specific good or service. Rates differ even within a category (e.g., 5% for economy flights vs. 12% for business class).
  • Ignoring CGST / SGST split — For intra-state transactions, the GST is split equally: 18% GST = 9% CGST + 9% SGST. For inter-state, it's 18% IGST. The total amount billed is the same, but the split matters for returns and input tax credit.
  • Not separating GST on invoices — Registered businesses must show the base amount, GST rate, and GST amount as separate line items on invoices. Lumping them together is non-compliant.
  • Forgetting input tax credit (ITC) — Businesses registered under GST can claim credit for GST paid on purchases. Failing to track this means paying more tax than legally required.

FAQ

Is GST charged on GST?

No. GST is always calculated on the base (pre-tax) value of a transaction. You never apply GST on top of GST — that would be double taxation and is not how the system works.

What is the difference between CGST, SGST, and IGST?

For sales within the same state (intra-state), GST is split equally between CGST (Central GST) and SGST (State GST). For sales across states (inter-state), the full GST is collected as IGST (Integrated GST). The total rate billed to the customer is the same in both cases.

How do I find the GST amount from a GST-inclusive price?

Use the formula: GST Amount = Inclusive Price − (Inclusive Price ÷ (1 + Rate / 100)). For example, ₹1,180 at 18% → GST = ₹1,180 − ₹1,000 = ₹180. Or just use the Remove GST option in the calculator.

Who needs to register for GST in India?

Businesses with an annual turnover above ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for service providers, ₹10 lakh for special category states) must register for GST. Certain businesses — like e-commerce sellers and inter-state suppliers — must register regardless of turnover.

Can I claim input tax credit (ITC) on all purchases?

Not always. ITC is available on business-related purchases, but is blocked for certain items like food and beverages, personal expenses, and motor vehicles (with some exceptions). Always verify eligibility before claiming ITC on a specific purchase category.
